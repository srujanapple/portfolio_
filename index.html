Object Detection

 2nd year

The project on "Object Detection Using OpenCV" involves leveraging the capabilities of the OpenCV (Open Source Computer Vision) library to implement an efficient system for detecting and recognizing objects in images or video streams. OpenCV provides a comprehensive set of tools and functions for image processing and computer vision, making it an ideal choice for tasks such as object detection. The project likely encompasses the use of pre-trained models like Haar cascades or deep learning-based models, such as YOLO (You Only Look Once) or SSD (Single Shot Multibox Detector), integrated with OpenCV to achieve accurate and real-time object detection. By combining advanced computer vision techniques with the versatility of OpenCV, the project aims to deliver a robust and adaptable solution for identifying objects in diverse visual contexts.

Plant leaf disease detection

 3rd year

In one of my compelling projects, we delved into the intriguing field of detecting plant leaf diseases during my academic endeavors. Envision a technology-driven solution capable of identifying and diagnosing diseases in plant leaves simply by analyzing their visual characteristics—that was our objective. Utilizing the capabilities of machine learning and computer vision, we developed a system capable of recognizing and categorizing various diseases affecting plant foliage. This entailed training models to differentiate between healthy and infected leaves, offering a prompt and accurate diagnosis. Witnessing the project progress from coding the algorithms to observing its practical impact on agriculture was genuinely fulfilling. This undertaking not only enhanced my comprehension of machine learning applications but also underscored the potential for technology to make significant contributions to sustainable farming practices. Exploring the convergence of technology and agriculture has been a rewarding aspect of my academic journey.
